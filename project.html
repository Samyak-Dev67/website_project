What's best for you

Common body shapes

Apple
Broader upper body and midsection with slimmer legs. Styling that creates vertical lines and light waist definition works well.
Pear
Narrower shoulders and wider hips. Emphasize the upper body with structured tops and eye-catching necklines.
Hourglass
Balanced bust and hips with a defined waist. Fitted waists and garments that follow curves flatter this silhouette.
Rectangle
More straight-lined silhouette with subtle curves. Use belts, layers and tailoring to add definition.

A reflection of shape and story

No body fits inside a single outline. We are sketches written by time, soft curves, sharp lines, quiet strengths. These shapes are not labels, but gentle metaphors, invitations to see yourself with kinder eyes. Let them guide you only as far as they spark curiosity; the rest of the canvas is yours to color.

Style is a conversation

Clothing isn’t a mask, it’s language. Every hemline, every silhouette whispers something about who you are, what you love, and how you move through the world. Let these shapes and tips be your vocabulary, not your rules. Wear what makes you feel awake, present, unmistakably yourself.
